Sleep Science

90-Minute Cycles

Each cycle includes light sleep, deep sleep, and REM sleep. Completing full cycles is key to feeling rested.

5-6 Cycles Ideal

Adults need 7-9 hours of sleep, which equals 5-6 complete cycles. Less than 4 cycles can lead to sleep deprivation.

Wake-Up Window

The best wake-up times are at the end of cycles. Even 10 minutes earlier can make you feel groggy all day.

Understanding Your Sleep

Sleep isn't just "shutting down" – it's an active process where your brain consolidates memories, your body repairs itself, and your mind processes emotions. The 90-minute cycle theory, first proposed by sleep researcher Nathaniel Kleitman, suggests that our sleep architecture follows predictable patterns throughout the night.

When you wake up during deep sleep (stages 3 and 4), you experience sleep inertia – that horrible groggy feeling that can last for hours. By timing your wake-up to the end of a cycle, you're more likely to be in light sleep or REM, making the transition to wakefulness smoother.
